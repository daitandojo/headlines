Ideas of the Week

Stop overlooking this FTSE 100 giant’s brilliant transformation

The events business has moved to a much better and more resilient strategy, but markets haven’t caught on
Published on August 7, 2025
by Valeria Martinez

Trade shows are booming again. Once dismissed as a relic in a world of Zoom calls and hybrid everything, physical events have stormed back to the centre of global dealmaking across industries from pharma and fintech to construction and cyber security. That has played directly into the hands of Informa (INF).

As one of the world’s largest exhibition organisers, the rapid bounceback of the former business-to-business (B2B) publisher has been more than just a return to normal. Informa’s revenues from events are now well over pre-pandemic levels, but the business itself has also changed along the way.

Over the past five years, the FTSE 100 giant has sold off smaller, less profitable operations and doubled down on live B2B events. Last year’s £1.2bn acquisition of Ascential brought in marquee festivals such as Money 20/20 for the fintech industry and Cannes Lions, which has been described as the Oscars of the marketing world.
